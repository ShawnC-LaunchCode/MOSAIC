Missouri Statutes

MOSAIC's classification logic is based on three Missouri statutes that restrict where certain individuals with qualifying sex offense convictions may be present. These laws establish a 500-foot proximity threshold from specific types of locations. This page summarizes each statute in plain language; the authoritative text is always the official Missouri Revised Statutes.

MOSAIC does not cover residence restrictions or registration requirements — only location-access rules relevant to recreation and public places.

Child Care Facilities

RSMo § 566.148 — View on revisor.mo.gov

Individuals convicted of qualifying offenses under Chapter 566 are prohibited from being physically present or loitering within 500 feet of any licensed child care facility.

MOSAIC: Flags any candidate location within 500 feet of a licensed childcare center as a result of this statute.

Penalty: Class A misdemeanor. Exception: parents, guardians, or custodians of a child enrolled at the facility.

School Property

RSMo § 566.149 — View on revisor.mo.gov

Individuals convicted of qualifying offenses are prohibited from being present within 500 feet of any elementary, middle, or high school building, school grounds, or school vehicle when minors are present.

MOSAIC: Flags any candidate location within 500 feet of a public or private K–12 school as a result of this statute.

Penalty: Class A misdemeanor. Exception: parents or guardians may attend school events with prior written permission from the superintendent, board, or principal.

Child-Focused Recreation & Public Facilities

RSMo § 566.150 — View on revisor.mo.gov

Individuals convicted of qualifying offenses are prohibited from being present or loitering within 500 feet of any of the following:

  • Public parks that contain a playground
  • Public swimming pools
  • Athletic facilities or complexes designed primarily for use by children
  • Children's museums
  • Missouri Department of Conservation nature and education centers

MOSAIC: Flags any candidate location within 500 feet of these facility types as a result of this statute.

Penalty: First violation — Class E felony. Subsequent violations — Class D felony. Exception: parents or guardians may receive written permission from the facility operator for their child's specific program.
